# Chapter 1: The silver secret

The sun climbed over the stone walls, painting the yard in morning gold. Marius stepped into the light. He was massive, a mountain of white fur that looked like a cloud had fallen from the sky and decided to stay.

When he spoke, his voice was a low rumble. It wasn't just a sound; it was a vibration that traveled through the dusty ground and tickled Cassia's paws.

"The sun is up," Marius said.

"The watch is on!" the cubs shouted together. Their voices bounced off the high walls, echoing like happy bells.

Marius looked down at them with eyes like polished amber. "A thief was here while you slept. My silver badge is gone. Find it."

Bella didn't wait. She was a streak of fur, dashing toward the heavy wooden gate. Archi scrambled under the long training bench, kicking up clouds of dust as he poked his nose into the shadows.

Cassia stayed still. She remembered what Marius had taught her. _The world is a puzzle,_ he had said. _You cannot see the pieces if you are running._

She closed her eyes and slowed her breath. _In and out. In and out._

When she opened them again, the yard was a collection of secrets. Shadows leaned against the wall. Tiny cracks appeared in the stone.

Then, she saw the dust.

A line of small, light prints marched across the yard. They were dainty and round, like little flower petals pressed into the dirt. They led straight toward the Big Gulp.

The Big Gulp was a clay jar so enormous it looked like it could swallow three cubs for breakfast and still have room for a snack. It sat in the corner, its wide mouth open to the sky, deep shadows hiding whatever was inside.

"Over here," Cassia whispered.

The other cubs gathered behind her as she followed the trail. The prints stopped right behind the giant jar. There, nestled in the dirt, lay the silver badge. It sparkled in the sun like a fallen piece of the moon.

"There!" Bella cried, pouncing on the silver. "I found it. We win."

"Wait," Cassia said. "The mystery isn't over."

Bella frowned, clutching the badge to her chest. "Why not? Marius said find the badge. I found it."

"We found the badge," Archi agreed, nodding his head.

Cassia knelt by the last footprint. "But we didn't find the thief. Look at the tracks. They come to the jar, but they don't lead away. The thief came here, but they never left."

Everyone went quiet. Silence filled the yard. The training bench and the gate stood exactly where they always had. But the Big Gulp seemed to watch them.

Arden stepped forward, a clever grin spreading across his face. He looked at Cassia, then tipped his ears toward the jar. "Suppose I was a thief," he murmured. "And suppose I didn't have time to run away. Where would I hide?"

He reached out and laid one paw on the cool, red clay of the jar.

The jar giggled.

A moment later, a tiny face popped over the rim. It was Lulu, the sand kitten. She was covered in dust and looked like a fuzzy dandelion.

"I stayed so very still," she squeaked, her eyes wide and bright. "I even held back a sneeze!"

Marius stepped closer, his shadow stretching over all of them. He looked at each cub in turn. "Bella, you were fast. Archi, you did exactly what I asked. But Cassia kept thinking after the work seemed done. And Arden understood how a thief might feel."

He looked down at the tiny kitten on the rim of the jar. "And you, my little shadow, stayed quieter than a mouse."

The heavy compound gate slammed open with a bang that shook the walls. Someone was running toward them, breathless and fast.
